# spin-freezing-mitigation-in-Ising-machines



Analog Ising machines solve combinatorial optimization problems by exploiting the collective
dynamics of interacting nonlinear physical systems. Their performance relies on a balance between
network-mediated interactions, which promote exploration of the energy landscape, and local non-
linear stabilization, which drives convergence toward low-energy states. When the local dynamics
dominate, however, the system can become trapped in metastable configurations, leading to spin
freezing and degraded optimization performance. Here, we investigate the emergence and control of
spin freezing across several analog Ising machine architectures, including the oscillator Ising machine,
dynamical Ising machine, and simulated bifurcation machine. We show that freezing arises from a
common dynamical mechanism despite architectural differences. To mitigate this effect, we introduce
controlled spatio-temporal heterogeneity in the local stabilizing term. Numerical simulations on
Erd˝os–R´enyi graphs demonstrate that this approach suppresses freezing, enhances exploration, and
significantly improves optimization performance across architectures.
